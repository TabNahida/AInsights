AInsights Index Methodology

AIndex is an alias for AInsights Index. Both names refer to the same default ranking method used on this site.

Benchmarks and Weights

AIndex uses the updated Artificial Analysis Intelligence Index evaluation-suite fields that are available in the public dataset, with the active weights below summing to 100%.

Benchmark Weight Role in AIndex
GDPval-AA v2 20% Agentic and economically grounded task performance.
τ³-Banking 14% Agentic banking workflow performance.
Terminal-Bench v2.1 16% Terminal-based coding and agentic execution.
SciCode 8% Scientific coding performance.
AA-LCR 6% Long-context reasoning.
AA-Omniscience Accuracy 12% The full Omniscience share is assigned to Accuracy.
Humanity's Last Exam 12% Broad expert-level reasoning.
GPQA Diamond 6% Graduate-level scientific question answering.
CritPt 6% Instruction-following and critical-point reasoning signal.

AA-Omniscience Non-Hallucination Rate remains visible elsewhere in the data, but Hallucination Rate is excluded from AIndex.

AIndex Calculation

  1. For each benchmark, convert the raw score into a Best score ratio: model score divided by the highest score observed for that benchmark.
  2. Apply the benchmark weights listed above. Missing benchmark values are counted as a zero ratio under the default AIndex preset.
  3. Aggregate the weighted ratios with Geometric Weighted Mean: exp(weighted average of log(ratio + 1)) - 1.
  4. Scale the resulting aggregate ratio by the highest AA Intelligence Index score available on the site, producing the displayed AIndex score.

Detailed Explanation

AIndex is designed to compare models across different benchmark scales without letting any single raw scoring convention dominate the final leaderboard. A percentage-style benchmark, an Elo-normalized field, and a long-context score can each have different ranges, so AIndex first places every active field on its own best-observed baseline.

The geometric weighted mean is used because it rewards balanced capability. A model that is very strong on one benchmark but missing or weak on another should not receive the same aggregate signal as a model that is consistently strong across the suite. The ratio + 1 transform keeps true zeroes and missing fields from collapsing the entire score to zero, while still penalizing them clearly.

After aggregation, AIndex is scaled back onto the AA Intelligence Index score range. This keeps the displayed numbers familiar while changing the underlying comparison from a raw weighted average to a relative-best, geometric-weighted method.

The Custom weights panel exposes the same building blocks for comparison: Best score ratio or Raw score, Geometric Weight Mean or regular Weight Mean, missing-value penalty strength, minimum coverage, and per-benchmark weights.
